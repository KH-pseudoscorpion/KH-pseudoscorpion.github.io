About

Kyung-Hoon Jeong
Ph.D. Student  ·  Leibniz Institute for the Analysis of Biodiversity Change, University of Hamburg

I study the phylogeography and diversity of pseudoscorpions across East Asia, investigating how the region's complex geological history has shaped the dispersal and evolutionary history of these small but ecologically significant arachnids.

My interests extend beyond pseudoscorpions, encompassing the distribution and evolutionary history of various invertebrates. I am particularly interested in applying integrative taxonomic approaches — combining morphological, molecular, and biogeographic data — to uncover cryptic diversity and understand speciation processes.
